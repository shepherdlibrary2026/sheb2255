🧭 Sermon & Study Method

A simple path for preparing biblical, clear, and practical teaching.

Read the text slowly

Write what the passage says before asking what you want it to say.

Study context

Check author, audience, setting, key words, and how the passage fits the book.

Compare Scripture

Use cross-references and related passages to guard against weak interpretation.

Build the main idea

Summarize the central truth in one clear sentence.

Apply to real life

Connect the truth to faith, family, leadership, holiness, mission, and worship.

Call for response

Invite people to obey God, trust Christ, repent, pray, serve, or grow.

🛡️ Research Safeguards

Keep every teaching biblical, humble, and responsible.

Always test outside articles, videos, sermon examples, commentaries, AI outputs, and social-media teachings by Scripture. Check context, doctrine, church accountability, and the spiritual needs of the audience before teaching publicly.

Do not build doctrine from one isolated verse

Confirm the teaching with the whole counsel of Scripture.

Separate Bible truth from personal opinion

Mark illustrations and application as support, not as the authority.

Serve the people in front of you

Pastors, youth, families, and leaders may need different examples but the same truth.
